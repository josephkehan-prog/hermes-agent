# Genre contracts

Load only the section matching the profile's primary genre.

## Romance — Heartline

- Center the relationship arc and guarantee an emotionally satisfying ending
  appropriate to the requested subgenre.
- Track attraction, vulnerability, conflict, trust, intimacy, and repair as
  separate ledger threads; do not use avoidable misunderstanding as filler.
- Give both leads independent wants, costs, agency, and meaningful choices.
- Test each major beat for chemistry, consent, emotional causality, and payoff.

## Fantasy — Spellbound

- Track world rules, costs, factions, geography, history, names, and magic
  constraints as canon IDs; wonder must not erase causality.
- Make setting pressure change decisions rather than serving as decoration.
- Foreshadow exceptions to rules before they solve major conflicts.
- Test every climax against established costs, promises, and character agency.

## Science fiction — Orbit

- Define the speculative premise, its constraints, and second-order social or
  personal consequences before plotting around it.
- Separate verified science, deliberate extrapolation, and invented rules.
- Prevent technology from becoming an unforeshadowed universal solution.
- Test plot turns for causal consistency and human stakes, not jargon density.

## Horror — Nocturne

- Define the fear engine, taboo, vulnerability, escalation ladder, and the
  boundary between shown and withheld information.
- Preserve uncertainty without breaking established evidence or point of view.
- Escalate through changed choices and consequences, not repeated shocks.
- Test the ending for thematic cost, lingering effect, and earned revelation.

## Mystery and thriller — Casefile

- Maintain a clue ledger with discovery chapter, discoverer, interpretation,
  truth value, dependencies, and payoff.
- Keep the solution available in retrospect; hide significance, not required
  evidence, unless the selected subgenre explicitly permits an unreliable text.
- Separate mystery fairness from thriller pressure: clues establish truth while
  deadlines, danger, and reversals create momentum.
- Run a final solution audit for means, motive, opportunity, causality, and red
  herrings that resolve cleanly.
